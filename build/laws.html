CAJM Constitution and By-Laws
Constitution and Bylaws for the Charleston Area Justice Ministry

Preamble

CAJM is an organization composed of religious congregations and other community organizations. Our member organizations, of diverse racial, religious, and economic backgrounds, pledge to work together to research and conduct public education to address the root causes of poverty and injustice, through the empowerment of marginalized people.

Article I

Incorporation

  1. The name of this organization shall be “Charleston Area Justice Ministry (CAJM)”.
  2. CAJM shall be a non-profit corporation, established in accordance with section 501 (c)(3) of the Tax Code of the Internal Revenue Service and the laws of the State of South Carolina.
  3. Authority is vested in the Annual Assembly (“Annual Assembly”), which is the ultimate decision-making body for the organization which delegates authority and responsibility between meetings to the Leadership Senate.

Article II

Affiliation with Direct Action and Research Training Center, Inc. (DART)

  1. CAJM shall maintain an affiliation with the Direct Action and Research Training Center, Inc. (DART).
  2. CAJM shall rely on DART to provide training and consulting support for CAISC leaders and to recruit and train new organizing staff.

Article III

Membership

  1. CAJM shall be an organization of congregations or community organizations. (hereafter referred to as congregation) with at least twenty (20) active members, subscribes to the goals and purposes of CAJM and supports the organization financially. (Individuals may volunteer with the organization and support the organization, but they may not hold membership.)
  2. Congregations become member organizations of CAJM after subscribing to the following covenant: _______ (name of the congregation), enters into a covenant with CAJM for the purpose of more successfully practicing our values in our community by:
    1. Building a Justice Ministry Network in our congregation;
    2. Engaging in a listening process, training leaders, developing solutions, and taking action to improve community conditions;
    3. Accepting responsibility for the financial health of the organization by contributing annually and by supporting CAJM fundraising efforts;
    4. Voicing the concerns of the members of our congregations during Assemblies and fully supporting the agreed upon goals; and finally...
    5. Selecting leaders (Justice Ministry Team members) from our congregation to attend all CAJM Leadership Senate meetings.
  3. Voting rights of member congregations at the Annual Assembly, Leadership Senate and Justice Ministry Team Assemblies shall depend upon the payment of dues in a timely manner.

Article IV

Annual Assembly

  1. All powers derive from and ultimate authority is invested in the Annual Assembly including the authority to:
    1. Adopt policies, set issue priorities, and authorize formation of issue/action committees;
    2. Elect the officers and Nominating Committee of CAJM;
    3. Amend this constitution in accordance with Article XII; and,
    4. Act on any other business of the organization properly brought before it.
  2. The Annual Assembly shall meet at least once a year on a date selected by the CAJM Leadership Senate. Meetings of the Annual Assembly may be called at any time by the Leadership Senate. Three-week’s written notice shall be given of any called meeting.
  3. Each member congregation shall send delegates to the Annual Meeting. Each member congregation shall be expected to send delegations composed of Justice Ministry Team and Justice Ministry Network Members.
    1. Only delegates who are present at the Annual Meeting shall have the right to vote. Each delegate present shall be entitled to one vote. A delegate shall represent only one organization.
    2. The Annual Assembly, excepting those delegations involved in any dispute regarding seat, voice or vote, shall, by a simple majority of voting members present, be the final judge in determining the seating of delegates and alternates.
  4. Notice of any Annual Meeting, including the agenda, shall be published to all member congregations at least two weeks prior to the meeting.
  5. The delegates present at any properly convened Annual Assembly shall constitute a quorum provided that at least one-half (1/2) of the member congregations have a delegate present.
  6. The Co-Presidents shall call the Annual Assembly to order and preside over the Assembly.

Article V

Staff

  1. CAJM staff shall consist of a Lead Organizer and other Organizer(s). Administrative personnel may also be employed.
  2. The Lead Organizer shall be accountable to the Board of Directors.
  3. The primary responsibilities of the Lead Organizer shall be as follow:
    1. Hire, supervise, mentor, and evaluate other staff employed by CAJM;
    2. Function as the primary liaison between CAJM and DART;
    3. Regularly seek out and build relationships with new leaders and facilitate bringing in new congregations;
    4. Identify potential sources of financial support;
    5. Ensure that all government and funding reports are completed in a timely fashion;
    6. Ensure that financial records are accurate and current;
    7. Develop and maintain a working knowledge of the issues, challenges, and local economic and political environment; and,
    8. Advise the Leadership Senate, the Board of Directors and committees.

Article VI

Officers and Board of Directors

  1. The Officers and the nine at Large members shall serve as the Board of Directors. The primary responsibilities of the members of the Board of Directors shall be to address the issues chosen by the Annual Assembly and communicate information from the Board meetings to the member congregations and information from the member congregations to the Board. The chief functions shall be as follow:
    1. Review the CAJM Assembly agendas and recommendations and plan for good attendance;
    2. Adopt the annual operating budget and secure and oversee the financial well-being of CAJM;
    3. Accept new member organizations;
    4. Act on the recommendations from the Membership Committee regarding applications of organizations applying for membership in CAJM;
    5. Implement the priorities adopted by the Annual Assembly;
    6. Communicate information regarding CAJM operations and issues to member congregations and represent congregational interests and concerns at Board meetings;
    7. Evaluate the CAJM ’s performance and accomplishments;
    8. Adopt a dues structure for the organization; and,
    9. Approve the employment of a Lead Organizer and establish guidelines for the evaluation of that person’s work.
  2. The Board of Directors shall meet at least six times per year.
  3. The Co-Presidents may convene special meetings of the Board of Directors. There shall be at least seven (7) days notice given to all Board members by mail/email or by phone. Special meetings must be called for stated, specific special business.
  4. Eight members of the Board shall be required to constitute a quorum for the Board of Directors. Participation in Board of Directors meetings may be by telephone or Skype when necessary.
  5. The officers of CAJM shall be:
    1. Co-Presidents (two), who shall be spokespeople for CAJM chair all CAJM meetings, including Assemblies, Senate meetings and Board meetings, and carry out all the other normal functions of president. The two Co-Presidents shall reflect the diversity of the organization and may be clergy or laypersons. The Co-Presidents will serve staggered two year terms;
    2. Vice-president, who shall assist the Co-Presidents as directed and in their absence act in their stead. The Vice-president will be elected for one term as Vice-President;
    3. Secretary, who shall record the proceedings of the Executive Committee, Assemblies, Leadership Senate and meetings of the Board of Directors. The Secretary will be elected for a one year term;
    4. Communications Officer, who shall be responsible for writing and posting notices and communications as directed by Co-Presidents and the Board of Directors, for providing notice of all meetings as set forth in this Constitution and Bylaws, for overseeing CAJM’s web presence and for developing media contacts. The Communications Officer will be elected for a one year term;
    5. Treasurer, who shall ensure that financial records are maintained in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles for not-for-profit organizations, submit a written financial report to the Board of Directors at the first meeting following the end of the previous quarter, and a full report to the Annual Assembly. The Treasurer shall submit all financial records annually for audit by the Finance Committee and for external audit when requested by the Board of Directors. The Treasurer will be elected for a one year term; and
    6. Development Officer, who shall oversee all funds collected by CAJM, monitor the payment of dues from member organizations, assist staff in membership recruitment, provide leadership in the development of corporate support of CAJM and assist in the formulation of fundraising strategies. The Development Officer will be elected for a one year term.
  6. Nine At-Large members of the Board of Directors shall be elected at the Annual Assembly for one year terms. (The first year that the at-large members are elected five shall be elected to two year terms.)
  7. The slate of officers and at-large Board members shall be presented by the Nominating Committee at least three weeks prior to the Annual Assembly and elected by a majority vote at the Annual Assembly. The Nominating Committee shall also nominate three at large members to serve on the Nominating Committee for the next year. The Nominating Committee shall nominate one qualified candidate for each office. Additional nominations may be offered provided that the nominee is supported by ten (10) signatures of delegates from at least five (5) member congregations and presented to the chairperson of the nominating committee one week prior to the Annual Assembly. The nominating committee shall consist of the current co-presidents and three at large members elected by the prior annual assembly.
  8. All candidates nominated by the nominating committee shall be members of a member organization and the slate of candidates shall reflect the rich diversity of the organization. Candidates for any position may be clergy or lay persons.
  9. Should an office become vacant, the Board of Directors shall have the power to recommend an acting successor to serve until the next Annual Assembly, whose appointment shall be ratified by the Leadership Senate at its next meeting. Full year vacancies shall be filled by the regular nomination process.
  10. Prior to the assumption of office, all Board Members shall take the following oath: I, ___________________ , do hereby solemnly affirm to uphold the Constitution and By-Laws of CAJM and to perform all duties as required by the Constitution and Bylaws and as mandated by the Annual Assembly.
  11. The Co-Presidents and Vice-President are limited to one term but may be elected to serve in another position. All other offices can be elected for a second term. Persons who have served their full allowed term(s) may be nominated for the same Board position after an absence from that position for one year.

Article VII

The Leadership Senate

  1. The Leadership Senate shall endeavor to build a culture of empowerment and accountability that engages all member congregations.
  2. The Leadership Senate shall consist of:
    1. The Board of Directors;
    2. The chairpersons of all standing committees and issue/action committees
    3. One Justice Ministry Team (JMT) Member from each congregation and an alternate, to be selected by the Clergyperson or JMT of the member congregation.
  3. All members of the Leadership Senate shall be active members in good standing of one of the member congregations of CAJM.
    4. The Lead Organizer shall be an ex-officio member of the Leadership Senate.
  4. The Lead Organizer shall be an ex-officio member of the Leadership Senate.
  5. The specific functions of the members of the Leadership Senate are to build a powerful Justice Ministry Network in their respective congregations, enlist additional Justice Ministry Team Members to assist in this process and communicate information from the Leadership Senate meetings to their respective congregations and information from their respective congregations to the Senate. Senate members will have responsibility to drive CAISC’s Strategic Plan to assure the following:
    1. A strong listening and network building process within the congregation,
    2. A turnout from the congregation for the Annual Assembly, the Nehemiah Action Assembly and other CAJM events,
    3. Fund raising efforts from each congregation in relationship to CAJM’s annual investment drive and payment of dues.
    4. To recommend the creation of new, or dissolution of existing, issue/action committees to the Annual Assembly,
    5. To conduct other business as may be directed by the Assemblies.
  6. The Leadership Senate shall meet at least four times per year.
  7. The Co-Presidents may convene special meetings of the Leadership Senate. There shall be at least seven (7) days notice given to all Senate members by mail or email or other writing. Special meetings must be called for specific business.
  8. Senate members or alternates from one half (1/2) of the member congregations shall constitute a quorum.

Article VIII

Justice Ministries Team Assemblies

  1. The Justice Ministry Team Assemblies shall consist of the Leadership Senate and members of each congregation/organization’s Justice Ministry Team. The chief functions of the Justice Ministry Team Assembly (hereafter referred to as Team Assembly) shall be:
    1. To implement the priorities adopted by the Annual Assembly
    2. To plan for a powerful turnout to Annual Assembly, rallies and actions:
    3. To ensure a successful Investment Drive and listening process;
    4. To welcome new congregations into membership.
  2. The Team Assembly shall meet at least four times a year on dates set by the Leadership Senate. Notice shall be provided to all member congregations at least 4 weeks ahead of time.
  3. A quorum of the Team Assembly shall consist of at least one Justice Ministry Team member from one-half (1/2) of all member congregations.

Article IX

On Committees

  1. There shall be the following Annual Assembly committees:
  2. There shall be the following standing committees of the Board of Directors:
    1. The Membership and Credentials Committee, which shall maintain a current membership list, identify congregations for recruitment for membership in CAJM and recommend appropriate action on membership applications to the Annual Assembly consistent with Article III of this Constitution, and review member organization participation in Assembly, Board, and other meetings and take action to increase participation or recommend removal from membership. The Development officer shall be an ex-officio member of this committee.
    2. The Finance Committee, which shall develop and recommend to the Board an annual budget and fund raising plan for CAJM, including the annual investment drive, annual membership dues and grant applications. The Treasurer shall be an ex-officio member of the Finance Committee.
    3. The Personnel Committee, shall assist in the employment, evaluation and supervision of the Lead Organizer and shall develop and recommend to the Board, personnel policies and procedures.
    4. New standing committees, as recommended by the Executive Committee and ratified by the Board of Directors Senate at its next meeting.
  3. There shall be the following Annual Assembly committees:
    1. The Nominating Committee, which shall nominate one qualified candidate for each vacancy on the Board of Directors and three at-large member of the next year’s nominating committee. . Additional nominations may be offered provided that the nominee is supported by ten (10) signatures of delegates from at least five (5) member congregations and presented to the chairperson of the nominating committee one week prior to the Annual Assembly. The Nominating Committee shall circulate a notice to all member congregations listing the positions that will be open and soliciting nominations. A list of all nominees, including background information on each, shall be circulated to each member congregation at least three weeks prior to the Annual Assembly. The Committee shall also keep a record of all nominations. In seeking and nominating candidates, the Committee shall seek to reflect the full diversity of CAJM. All discussions about potential candidates held during nominating committee meetings shall be held strictly confidential. The nominating committee shall consist of all the current Co-Presidents and three at large members elected by the annual assembly.
    2. The Elections Committee shall be responsible for the conduct of elections under the direction of the Annual Assembly chair. This committee shall also be responsible for conducting the voting at the Annual Assembly and other Annual Assembly business subject to the direction of the Annual Assembly chair.
  4. Action Committees may be created by the Annual Assembly.
    1. CAJM issue/action committees shall research and develop winnable solutions for community problems and shall monitor implementation of community improvements. They shall dissolve when their specific goals have been achieved or at the discretion of the Board.
    2. Chairpersons of CAJM issue/action committees shall be appointed by the Co-Presidents and approved by the Board of Directors.
    3. These committees shall report to the Board of Directors and Leadership Senate until their work is completed.
    4. Chairpersons of issue/action committees shall be active leaders of one of the member congregations of CAJM.
    5. Membership on the action committee shall be open to any active leaders in good standing of one of the member congregations (or potential member congregation) of CAJM who, supports the goals of the organization, are willing to work on the issue, and committed to turning out members from their congregation to The Nehemiah Action and other Assemblies called by the organization.

Article X

Non-Partisan and Non-Discrimination Policies

  1. Under no circumstances shall CAJM endorse any candidate or slate of candidates for public office.
  2. The Organization shall not ally itself with, nor allow its name to be used to endorse any candidate for political office, any political party or any political action committee.
  3. Membership in or participation in the activities of CAJM shall not be determined in any way by gender, color, race, religion, national origin, disability, or citizenship.
  4. No Board Member may hold elected public office or be a candidate for such.

Article XI

On the Misconduct of Officers, Representatives and Committee Chairs

  1. An officer, representative or committee chairs may be dismissed on the grounds of misconduct in office.
  2. Misconduct charges shall be submitted in writing to the Co-Presidents (or in the case of a Co-President being charged, the vice-president), who shall allow the person accused to respond in writing and undertake any other appropriate investigation. The investigator will bring his or her recommendation before the Board of Directors at its next meeting. The investigator may request a reasonable extension of time.
  3. After due consideration, a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the members of the Board of Directors at a legally constituted meeting shall be required to remove a person from office.

Article XII

Amendments to the Constitution and Bylaws

  1. This Constitution and Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the delegates present at the Annual Assembly, provided a quorum is present.
  2. Any proposed amendments to the Constitution and Bylaws shall be forwarded to the Board of Directors at least 60 days prior to any meeting at which they would be considered.
  3. Any amendment of the Constitution and Bylaws recommended by the Board of Directors shall be distributed to the delegates to the Annual Assembly at least three weeks days prior to any meeting at which the amendments would be considered.

Article XIII

On the Conduct of Business

  1. Robert’s Rules of Order, Revised (most recent edition), shall be the authority to decide all questions of order for the conduct of meetings of CAJM, unless otherwise provided for in this Constitution and Bylaws.
  2. The Annual Assembly may establish other rules of order for the conduct of business for their meetings, provided that a majority of the voting members present are in accord.
  3. Meetings of the Annual Assembly and the Leadership Senate shall begin with an appropriate reflection upon the ideals and mission of CAJM.
  4. All minutes of the Annual Assembly, Board, and committee meetings, along with financial records and official correspondence, shall be kept in the office of the organization.

Article XIV

Indemnification and Insurance

To the fullest extent not prohibited by applicable law, the organization shall indemnify each person, who by reason of being or having been a Director or officer of the organization, named or otherwise, becomes or is threatened to be made a party to any legal, judicial, administrative or other proceeding, and the organization by the Board of Directors may indemnify any other person as deemed proper by the Board, against any and all costs and expenses (including attorney fees, judgments, fines, penalties, amounts paid in settlement, and other disbursements) actually and reasonably incurred by or imposed upon such person in connection with any action, suit, investigation or proceeding (or claim or other matter therein), whether civil, criminal, administrative or otherwise in nature, with respect to which such person is named or otherwise in nature, with respect to which such person is named or otherwise threatened to be made a party by reason of being or at any time having been a Director, officer, employee or other agent of or in a similar capacity with the organization, or by reason of being or at any time having been, at the direction or request of the organization, a director, officer, administrator, manager, employee, member, advisor or other agent of or fiduciary for any other corporation, partnership, trust, venture or other entity or enterprise including any employee benefit plan.

Article XV

Disposition of Assets

Upon dissolution of this corporation, the remaining assets of the corporation shall be transferred to one or more corporations, societies, or organizations engaged in activities substantially similar to those of this corporation, and as shall at the time qualify as exempt organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1954 (or corresponding provision of any future United States Internal Revenue Code or Law).

Approved at the Annual Assembly, ______________________
